1.0 Project Description
Many San José State University students face daily transportation challenges, including limited campus parking, high gas costs, traffic congestion, and inconsistent public transportation schedules. While commercial ride-sharing services exist, they are often expensive and not restricted to the campus community, which can raise safety and trust concerns. Additionally, students who are willing to carpool often lack a centralized and reliable platform to connect with other SJSU students. UniRide is being built to address these problems by providing a secure, university-only carpool coordination system. The platform connects SJSU students who need rides with verified student drivers who have available seats. By limiting access to registered SJSU students and implementing driver verification, UniRide promotes safety, accountability, and trust within the campus community. The system streamlines ride coordination through searchable ride listings, booking functionality, and automated seat management. By reducing the number of single-occupancy vehicles on campus, UniRide aims to lower commuting costs, ease parking demand, and support more sustainable transportation practices at SJSU.

3.0 Functional Requirements:
Users can register and log in to UniRide as a passenger, a driver, or both. All users must be SJSU students with a SJSU ID. Drivers must provide a license number and will have their status pending until verified, they also have the ability to add vehicles to their profile. Drivers must choose a departure location for all the passengers to come to. All users can log in, log out, change their passwords, and delete their accounts. Passengers can look at and are able to search for available rides. They will be able to filter results based on information such as destination and departure time. Passengers will be able to view ride details and book the ride. Passengers will also have the option to cancel the booking, granted that some conditions are met, the most important being that the ride cannot have already left. The booking system automatically updates seat availability when a booking or cancellation occurs. It prevents overbooking and blocks reservations if a ride is full, cancelled, completed, or already departed. Rides automatically change to “Completed” status after the scheduled departure time.

4.2 Security:
The security of UniRide will be built into the core of the application. To ensure a safe environment for all campus users, UniRide will be implementing the following security measures:
The system will use a role-based access control approach to ensure that only verified drivers will have access to driver-related tools, such as creating a ride. Passengers will not be able to modify any ride data and will only be allowed to view and book a seat on a ride. 
The system will be designed to protect and secure users' credentials, such as passwords and personal information.
Secure authentication, such as user verification, will be implemented where users must provide their current password to make sensitive changes.
4.3 Access Control:
All users must log in with a valid account to access UniRide. Guest access is not permitted. Access privileges are determined by the user’s role selected during registration and verified by the system. UniRide supports two primary roles: Passenger and Driver (users may have both roles if desired).
4.3.1 General Access:
All registered users can:
Users will be required to log in with a valid username and password combination.
Authorized users can log out anytime
Users can change their passwords.
Changing the password requires software to check password strength and to prevent the user from changing to the current password.
Users can delete their own accounts.
The interface will allow switching between passenger and driver dashboards, allowing the enforcement of appropriate permissions.
4.3.2 Passenger Access:
Passengers have limited permissions focused on searching and booking rides. They cannot modify ride data or driver information.
Passengers can:
View all available rides
Filter rides by destination, departure time, and availability
View ride details (driver name, vehicle information, seats available, locations)
Request or book seats on rides
Cancel their ride bookings
View ride status (open, full, completed, cancelled)
View personal ride history
Passengers cannot:
Create, edit, or delete rides
Access other passengers’ personal information
4.3.3 Driver Access:
Newly registered drivers are pending until approved. Driver privileges are granted only after driver eligibility is verified.
Verified drivers can:
Add, edit, and delete their vehicles
Provide vehicle details (make, model, color, license plate, seat capacity)
Create rides with pickup location, destination, date, time, and available seats
Accept or manage passenger requests for their rides
Cancel the rides they created
View passengers booked on their rides
Manage ride status and seat availability
View their driving history
Drivers cannot:
Modify rides created by other drivers
Access sensitive information unrelated to themselves
